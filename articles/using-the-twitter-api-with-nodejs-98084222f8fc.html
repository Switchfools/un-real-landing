Reducing risk during earthquakes

We have developed a safer AI controller to increase building safeness during earthquakes. This controller reduces the expected damage by 66%. Our AI controller also presents data efficiency as it requires only 4 seconds of seismic data for tuning the controller, leading to significant risk reduction in multiple seismic scenarios.

Humans have continuously developed civil structures from ancient times to supply their own needs. Today some buildings, called active structures, present advantages increasing the structure's safeness by reducing the risk of collapse during an earthquake. These structures contain sensors and actuators that modify the building's response to its environment. These actuators, like active dampers or active tendons, apply forces to adjust the structure's shape. Nonetheless, how to optimally control these dampers still being an open question.

In this area, the vast majority of the developed algorithms are based on analytical models, as PIDs, that predict the action to reduce structure's damage. However, these strategies are obsolete. The high complexity of nonlinear systems and the probabilistic behavior of the environment are intractable for humans. Applying this methodology to real structures is even more intricate because buildings change over time. Therefore, Machine Learning could perform a fundamental role in risk reduction for buildings.
